# SAFE-K8S-0905-014 - AI workload circuit-breaker and emergency halt mechanisms

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0905-036

## Statement

Organizations must implement circuit-breaker mechanisms that can automatically or manually halt AI workloads (training jobs, inference services, pipeline stages) when predefined safety or security thresholds are breached. Circuit-breakers must cover anomalous resource consumption, model output quality degradation, data pipeline integrity failures, and security policy violations. Emergency halt must be achievable within a defined maximum response time and must preserve forensic evidence.

## Objective

Enable rapid containment of AI workloads that exhibit anomalous, unsafe, or compromised behavior by providing reliable circuit-breaker and emergency halt capabilities.

## Rationale

AI workloads can fail in ways that are difficult to detect through traditional monitoring: training runs may silently produce poisoned models, inference services may begin returning adversarially manipulated outputs, or pipelines may process tampered data. Without circuit-breaker mechanisms, these failures continue unchecked, potentially causing cascading damage. Emergency halt capabilities are also required by regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act Article 14) for human oversight of high-risk AI systems.

## Scope

Applies to all AI workloads running on Kubernetes infrastructure, including training jobs, inference services, and data pipelines. Covers automated circuit-breakers triggered by threshold violations and manual emergency halt procedures invokable by authorized operators.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Workload controller configuration (Job termination, Deployment scaling to zero, service mesh circuit-breaking) and operator runbook procedures.
- Implementation Surface: Kubernetes Job activeDeadlineSeconds and backoffLimit, HPA/KEDA scale-to-zero triggers, service mesh circuit-breaker configuration (Istio OutlierDetection, Envoy circuit-breaking), custom operator health checks with automated remediation, operator emergency halt runbooks, and forensic evidence preservation procedures (pod logs, metrics snapshots, artifact checksums before termination).
- Owner Primary: AI/ML Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Operations; SRE

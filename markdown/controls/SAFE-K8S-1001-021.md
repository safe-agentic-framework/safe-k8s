# SAFE-K8S-1001-021 - Centralized log integrity protection and retention enforcement for AI workloads

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must protect centralized AI workload logs against tampering through cryptographic verification or append-only immutability controls and must enforce retention periods aligned with organizational and regulatory requirements for each applicable log class.

## Objective

Preserve trustworthy and durable AI workload log evidence by enforcing tamper resistance and retention controls on centralized logging backends.

## Rationale

Centralized collection alone does not preserve evidentiary value if logs can be altered, truncated, or deleted prematurely. Integrity protection and retention enforcement ensure that AI workload log evidence remains trustworthy and available for incident response, audit, and regulatory record-keeping.

## Scope

Applies to centralized logging backends and retained AI workload log classes, including audit logs, training and inference logs, GPU hardware logs, and platform security telemetry retained for operational or regulatory purposes.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Centralized log backend immutability, integrity verification, and retention lifecycle enforcement.
- Implementation Surface: Append-only or WORM-compliant storage, signed log batches or hash-chain integrity verification, immutable object storage controls, retention policy configuration per log class, and periodic validation that retained logs remain retrievable for audit and investigation.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Infrastructure Engineering

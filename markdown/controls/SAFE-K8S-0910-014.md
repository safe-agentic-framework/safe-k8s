# SAFE-K8S-0910-014 - Cross-cluster registry replication channel authentication and endpoint enrollment

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Model registry replication across clusters must use authenticated and encrypted replication channels, and only explicitly enrolled source and destination registry endpoints may participate in federation or replication workflows.

## Objective

Prevent interception, spoofing, and unauthorized federation joins during cross-cluster model registry replication by requiring cryptographically authenticated transport and governed endpoint enrollment.

## Rationale

A trusted source registry does not prevent tampering if the replication path can be intercepted or if an unapproved endpoint can join the federation. Authenticated transport and endpoint enrollment controls reduce the risk of rogue registries receiving, injecting, or modifying replicated model artifacts across cluster boundaries.

## Scope

Applies to model registry replication and federation channels spanning clusters or regions, including Harbor replication, OCI registry replication, and custom model distribution pipelines where registry endpoints exchange replicated artifacts.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Replication channel configuration and federation endpoint identity validation at each participating registry endpoint.
- Implementation Surface: mTLS or equivalent authenticated replication channels, SPIFFE or other workload identity for replication endpoints, endpoint allowlists or federation enrollment approvals, and operational controls that block unauthenticated or unapproved registry peers.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering

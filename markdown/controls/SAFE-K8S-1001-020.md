# SAFE-K8S-1001-020 - Cluster-wide AI workload log collection and centralized aggregation

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must collect node-level and container logs from all Kubernetes nodes and AI workloads, including managed-service and GPU-specific log sources where applicable, and aggregate those logs into a centralized logging platform for security monitoring and investigation.

## Objective

Ensure AI workload logs remain available for monitoring and investigation by deploying comprehensive cluster-wide collection and centralized aggregation across Kubernetes and AI-specific execution surfaces.

## Rationale

Distributed Kubernetes and AI platforms emit security-relevant logs from ephemeral pods, nodes, managed control planes, and GPU subsystems. Without comprehensive collection and aggregation, critical evidence can be lost during node recycling, pod eviction, or provider-managed platform transitions, reducing detection and investigation fidelity.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads in production and pre-production environments. Includes node-level log agents, provider-generated cluster logs, centralized log aggregation services, and AI workload log streams such as training, inference, and GPU device logs.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Node-level collection agent deployment and centralized log aggregation platform onboarding for all relevant cluster log sources.
- Implementation Surface: DaemonSet-based log collectors on all nodes including GPU nodes with taint tolerations, provider log export integration, container and kubelet log collection paths, GPU driver and device log capture, and centralized aggregation platforms such as OpenSearch, Loki, Splunk, or equivalent services.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Infrastructure Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-1005-012 - AI-specific forensic preservation of GPU, model access, and training provenance evidence

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must extend forensic evidence collection for Kubernetes incidents to preserve AI-specific evidence, including GPU allocation and accelerator state logs, model access audit trails, and training data or pipeline provenance records needed to reconstruct AI workload compromise or misuse.

## Objective

Preserve AI-specific forensic evidence that would otherwise be lost during Kubernetes incident response so investigators can determine whether models, accelerators, or training data were impacted.

## Rationale

AI workloads produce volatile evidence beyond standard Kubernetes artifacts. GPU allocation state, model access history, and training data provenance can disappear quickly or become inconsistent if not preserved during response. Dedicated AI-specific collection procedures are necessary to determine whether a model was tampered with, a GPU workload was abused, or training data lineage was affected.

## Scope

Applies to Kubernetes clusters running GPU workloads, model registries, experiment tracking systems, and training or inference pipelines where AI-specific evidence is needed for incident reconstruction and post-incident decisions.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: AI-specific forensic playbooks and evidence capture procedures for GPU, model, and training-data artifacts.
- Implementation Surface: GPU telemetry and allocation evidence capture, model registry or model CRD access trail preservation, training pipeline execution and data lineage evidence collection, artifact hashing, and storage of AI-specific forensic outputs in the evidence repository.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Digital Forensics; AI/ML Engineering; Legal

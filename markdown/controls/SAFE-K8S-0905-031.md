# SAFE-K8S-0905-031 - Internal re-signing, external-origin annotation, and registry admission for external models

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must internally re-sign, annotate, and re-register approved external models before they are deployable within organizational environments.

## Objective

Convert approved external artifacts into internally governed assets with organizational signatures, lineage annotations, and registry admission records.

## Rationale

Using externally sourced models directly from outside repositories breaks the organization's trust boundary. Internal re-signing, ML-BOM external-origin annotation, and internal registry enrollment create a controlled chain of custody and ensure only internally governed artifacts become deployable.

## Scope

Applies to externally sourced models approved for internal use. Covers internal artifact signing, ML-BOM annotation of external origin and scan results, and internal registry enrollment records. Excludes source allowlist enforcement and pre-admission quarantine scanning.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Internal signing and registry admission process.
- Implementation Surface: Internal artifact signing, ML-BOM annotation of external origin and scan results, and internal registry enrollment records for approved external models before deployment eligibility.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

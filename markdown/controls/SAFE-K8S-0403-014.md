# SAFE-K8S-0403-014 - Automated secret leak detection coverage and enforcement

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.3 - Secrets Management
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must deploy and enforce automated secret leak detection across source repositories, pre-commit workflows, container images, CI/CD artifacts, runtime logs, and log aggregation systems to identify credential exposure before or immediately after publication.

## Objective

Ensure credential leaks are automatically detected across the artifact and runtime surfaces where they commonly appear, reducing dwell time and preventing unnoticed exposure.

## Rationale

Secrets can be exposed in commits, image layers, pipeline artifacts, and logs. Broad automated scanning and enforced prevention points such as pre-commit or push protection reduce the chance that leaked credentials remain undetected.

## Scope

Applies to source repositories, developer workflows, container image registries, CI/CD artifacts, runtime logs, and centralized log platforms where credentials may be exposed. Covers scanning coverage, enforcement points, and alert generation for detected leaks.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Automated secret scanning and enforcement controls in developer, CI/CD, artifact, and logging platforms.
- Implementation Surface: Secret scanning in repositories and pre-commit or push protection, image and artifact scanning, CI/CD secret detection gates, runtime log scanning, and log aggregation pattern detection with alert routing.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Application Development

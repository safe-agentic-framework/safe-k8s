# SAFE-K8S-0910-002 - Cross-cluster model registry federation and replication security

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Model registry federation and replication across clusters must enforce signature verification at each replication endpoint, must use authenticated and encrypted replication channels, must maintain provenance chain-of-custody across cluster boundaries, and must prevent unauthorized registry endpoints from joining the federation.

## Objective

Ensure that model artifacts replicated across clusters maintain their integrity, provenance, and authenticity throughout the replication process, preventing supply chain attacks through compromised replication channels.

## Rationale

Model registry replication creates a supply chain attack surface at each federation point. A compromised replication endpoint can inject tampered model artifacts into downstream clusters. Without signature re-verification at pull time, a downstream cluster trusts the source registry's assertion rather than independently verifying model integrity. Without provenance chain-of-custody, models cannot be traced back to their originating training pipeline after replication.

## Scope

Applies to organizations replicating model registries across multiple Kubernetes clusters or cloud regions. Covers Harbor replication, MLflow registry federation, OCI artifact replication, and custom model distribution pipelines. Single-cluster registry hardening is addressed by SAFE-K8S-0905-003.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Registry replication configuration, federation membership management, signature verification policies at pull time, and provenance metadata preservation settings.
- Implementation Surface: Registry replication configuration (Harbor replication, MLflow registry federation, OCI artifact replication), cosign signature re-verification at pull time on each destination cluster, mTLS with SPIFFE-based identity for replication channels, provenance metadata preservation settings (training pipeline attestation, signing identity, promotion gate results, originating cluster), content-addressable digest verification after replication, periodic reconciliation scans detecting cross-endpoint drift, and federation membership governance with change management procedures.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering

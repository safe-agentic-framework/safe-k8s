# SAFE-K8S-0905-013 - Approved external model source allowlist enforcement

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0905-026

## Statement

Organizations must maintain a curated allowlist of approved external model sources, must enforce the allowlist through NetworkPolicy blocking direct pulls from public hubs to production namespaces, and must deploy admission controllers that reject pods pulling models from unapproved external sources.

## Objective

Restrict external model ingestion to approved sources and prevent direct pulls from unapproved public model hubs into production infrastructure.

## Rationale

Without an enforced allowlist, engineers can pull models from any public source, including typosquatted or impersonated repositories distributing malicious models under trusted names. Network-level and admission control enforcement ensures the allowlist cannot be bypassed.

## Scope

Applies to all external model sources accessed from Kubernetes infrastructure. Covers the curated allowlist of approved sources, NetworkPolicy enforcement blocking unauthorized hub access, and admission controller validation of model source URLs. Excludes the quarantine and vetting workflow covered by SAFE-K8S-0905-008.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: NetworkPolicy blocking direct public hub access from production namespaces and admission controllers rejecting pods that pull models from unapproved external sources.
- Implementation Surface: Approved external model source allowlist (repository URLs, organization identifiers), NetworkPolicy blocking direct public hub access from production namespaces, admission controllers (Kyverno, Gatekeeper) rejecting pods pulling from unapproved external sources, and periodic allowlist review and update procedures.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Governance

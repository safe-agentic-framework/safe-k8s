# SAFE-K8S-0104-010 - Recurring CIS Kubernetes Benchmark assessment

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.4 - CIS Benchmarks and Patch Management
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Production Kubernetes clusters must be assessed against the CIS Kubernetes Benchmark using automated tooling (kube-bench or equivalent) on a recurring schedule, including the applicable managed-service benchmark profile where relevant, and results must be retained to demonstrate baseline hardening posture over time.

## Objective

Establish a measurable, repeatable Kubernetes hardening baseline by running recurring automated CIS Benchmark assessments and retaining evidence of posture trends.

## Rationale

Recurring benchmark assessment detects configuration drift across control-plane, node, and policy surfaces before gaps accumulate into exploitable exposure. Without scheduled assessment and retained results, baseline hardening claims cannot be verified over time.

## Scope

Applies to all production clusters. Covers CIS Benchmark sections for control-plane components, worker nodes, and policies. For managed Kubernetes services, applies to the customer-assessable managed benchmark profile and to recording provider-managed checks that cannot be directly evaluated.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Audit / Telemetry
- Implementation Surface: Automated CIS Benchmark scanning pipeline, scheduled jobs, and posture reporting workflow.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Governance Risk and Compliance

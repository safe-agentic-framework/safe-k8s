# SAFE-K8S-0401-005 - Separation of duties for ML, platform, and security roles

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.1 - Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must enforce separation of duties between ML engineers (model deployment and training), platform engineers (cluster configuration and infrastructure), and security teams (policy enforcement and audit) through distinct RBAC roles, and must restrict GPU resource management permissions including GPU quota requests, scheduling priority overrides, and GPU metrics access to authorized roles only.

## Objective

Prevent any single role from spanning the full lifecycle of AI workloads from infrastructure provisioning through model deployment to policy enforcement, reducing the risk of unauthorized or unreviewed changes to sensitive AI infrastructure.

## Rationale

Without separation of duties, an ML engineer could modify cluster-level security policies, or a platform engineer could deploy untested models to production. In GPU-enabled environments, unrestricted access to GPU quotas and scheduling priorities allows resource abuse, cost overruns, or denial of service to legitimate training jobs. Clear role boundaries ensure that model deployment, infrastructure changes, and security policy modifications each require distinct authorization.

## Scope

Applies to all clusters running AI/ML workloads with GPU resources. Covers RBAC for core Kubernetes resources, AI operator CRDs, GPU ResourceQuota objects, PriorityClass resources, and GPU monitoring endpoints. Exceptions require documented risk acceptance.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Distinct RBAC Roles and ClusterRoles per organizational function (ML engineering, platform engineering, security), enforced through RoleBindings mapped to identity provider groups.
- Implementation Surface: Distinct RBAC Roles and ClusterRoles per organizational function (ML Engineer, Platform Engineer, Security Engineer), RoleBindings mapped to identity provider groups, GPU ResourceQuota RBAC restrictions, PriorityClass resource RBAC controls, GPU metrics endpoint access policies, and identity provider group-to-role mapping configurations for separation of model deployment, cluster configuration, and policy enforcement permissions.
- Owner Primary: Identity and Access Management
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; ML Engineering; Security Engineering

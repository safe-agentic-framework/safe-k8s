# SAFE-K8S-0403-008 - Credential inventory and sprawl governance at scale

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.3 - Secrets Management
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations running AI workloads at scale must maintain an automated credential inventory tracking all active secrets, tokens, and keys issued to AI workloads, implement orphaned credential detection for completed or failed jobs, monitor credential scope drift, and publish credential lifecycle metrics to enable governance of credential sprawl across the platform.

## Objective

Provide visibility and governance over the volume, scope, and lifecycle of credentials issued to AI workloads, preventing credential sprawl where thousands of orphaned or over-scoped credentials accumulate undetected across the platform.

## Rationale

AI platforms operating at scale generate thousands of credentials daily -- each training job, inference server, notebook session, and pipeline step may receive multiple credentials for data access, model registry, feature store, and compute resources. Without automated inventory and governance, orphaned credentials from completed jobs persist indefinitely, credential scopes drift broader over time as teams request exceptions, and security teams lose visibility into the platform's credential surface area. SAFE-K8S-0402-002 addresses stale service account remediation; this control extends that principle to the full credential inventory at AI platform scale with automated detection, scope monitoring, and lifecycle metrics.

## Scope

Applies to all credentials issued to AI/ML workloads including Vault dynamic secrets, cloud IAM temporary credentials, Kubernetes service account tokens, API keys for external services, and model registry authentication tokens. Covers credential inventory automation, orphaned credential detection, scope drift monitoring, and governance dashboards.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Credential inventory system with automated reconciliation against workload state (running, completed, failed), scope drift detection comparing granted vs. used permissions, and alerting on credential lifecycle violations.
- Implementation Surface: Vault audit logs and lease inventory, cloud IAM credential reports, Kubernetes service account token tracking, pipeline orchestrator job state correlation, credential governance dashboards.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; ML Engineering; Cloud Platform Engineering

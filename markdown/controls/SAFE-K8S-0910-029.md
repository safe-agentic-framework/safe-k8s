# SAFE-K8S-0910-029 - Centralized multi-cluster secret, certificate, and incident governance coordination

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters must coordinate centralized secret governance, certificate lifecycle governance, and incident response operations across the federation so critical security operations are managed consistently across cluster boundaries.

## Objective

Coordinate high-impact governance operations across multi-cluster AI platforms so secret, certificate, and incident handling do not fragment by cluster.

## Rationale

Even with consistent policy and compliance reporting, governance fails when secrets and certificates are managed inconsistently across clusters or incidents are handled cluster by cluster without federation-wide coordination. Centralized multi-cluster governance operations reduce synchronization gaps and ensure cross-cluster response authority, timing, and lifecycle controls remain coherent.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters. Covers centralized secret governance, certificate lifecycle coordination, and multi-cluster incident coordination across the federation. Excludes unified compliance reporting and evidence aggregation.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Coordinated governance operations process for secrets, certificates, and multi-cluster incidents.
- Implementation Surface: Centralized secret management with per-cluster authentication, coordinated certificate lifecycle governance across clusters, and multi-cluster incident response procedures, escalation paths, and exercises.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; GRC/Compliance; ML Platform Engineering

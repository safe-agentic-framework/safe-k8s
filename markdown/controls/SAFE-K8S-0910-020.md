# SAFE-K8S-0910-020 - Cross-cluster transport encryption for distributed AI traffic

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads must use encrypted transport channels so gradient exchange, inference routing, and pipeline data transfer cannot be intercepted or tampered with across cluster boundaries.

## Objective

Protect cross-cluster AI data in transit from interception and tampering by enforcing encrypted transport across federated clusters.

## Rationale

Distributed AI traffic often crosses network paths and trust boundaries that single-cluster controls cannot protect. Transport encryption prevents eavesdropping and tampering on cross-cluster links before higher-level identity, authorization, or workload logic is evaluated.

## Scope

Applies to all cross-cluster network communication for AI workloads, including distributed training gradient exchange, multi-cluster inference routing, and cross-cluster pipeline data transfer. Covers encrypted transport channels across cluster boundaries. Excludes endpoint identity authentication and authorization decisions for those channels.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Encrypted transport configuration for cross-cluster links.
- Implementation Surface: Multi-cluster service mesh mTLS encryption, VPN or WireGuard tunnel encryption, certificate-based transport protection for cross-cluster AI data paths, and verification of encrypted transport on cross-cluster links.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; ML Platform Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-1005-004 - Ransomware and destructive attack response for Kubernetes

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must maintain ransomware and destructive attack response procedures for Kubernetes environments that include regular backup verification (including etcd snapshots), documented etcd restoration procedures, workload recovery prioritization based on business criticality, and post-incident lessons-learned processes that feed back into preparedness improvements.

## Objective

Ensure that Kubernetes environments can recover from ransomware or destructive attacks with minimal data loss and downtime through validated backup, tested restoration procedures, and structured post-incident improvement.

## Rationale

Ransomware targeting Kubernetes clusters can encrypt etcd, destroy persistent volumes, or corrupt container images in registries, rendering the entire cluster and its AI workloads unrecoverable without tested backups. Without verified etcd restoration procedures, organizations discover backup failures during recovery. Without recovery prioritization, critical AI inference services may be restored after lower-priority workloads, extending business impact. Without lessons-learned processes, the same attack vectors remain exploitable.

## Scope

Applies to all production Kubernetes clusters and their supporting infrastructure (etcd, container registries, persistent storage). Covers backup verification, restoration testing, recovery prioritization, and post-incident review. Must include AI workload recovery considerations such as model artifact restoration and training checkpoint recovery.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Backup management system, disaster recovery runbook repository, and post-incident review process.
- Implementation Surface: Etcd snapshot scheduling and test restoration procedures (etcdctl snapshot save/restore, kube-apiserver stop/restart sequence), PersistentVolume backup to retention-locked storage for model artifacts and training data, container registry replication for image availability, workload recovery prioritization framework (control plane, logging/monitoring, production inference, active training, pipeline infrastructure, development workloads), documented etcd restoration runbook (stop apiserver, restore snapshot, update etcd config, restart, verify with kubectl get nodes/pods), and post-incident lessons-learned process feeding back into playbook, containment, and audit policy updates.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; SRE; AI/ML Engineering

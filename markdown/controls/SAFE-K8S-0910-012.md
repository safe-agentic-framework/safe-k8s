# SAFE-K8S-0910-012 - Multi-cluster security policy federation and drift remediation

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters must enforce consistent security policies and configuration baselines across all clusters in the federation and must detect and remediate policy drift between clusters.

## Objective

Prevent exploitable inconsistencies between clusters in a multi-cluster AI platform by enforcing uniform policy and configuration posture with continuous drift remediation.

## Rationale

When clusters are managed inconsistently, attackers target the weakest cluster with the least restrictive admission controls, network policies, RBAC rules, or AI operator configurations. Policy federation and drift remediation reduce those inconsistencies before they become exploitable.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters. Covers centralized policy distribution, baseline consistency, and continuous drift detection and remediation across clusters. Excludes unified compliance reporting, centralized secret and certificate governance, and multi-cluster incident coordination, which are covered by SAFE-K8S-0910-013.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Centralized policy distribution system and drift detection monitoring.
- Implementation Surface: Policy-as-code frameworks with centralized Git-based policy distribution to all clusters, continuous monitoring for drift in admission policies, NetworkPolicy, RBAC, security contexts, and AI operator configuration, plus remediation workflows for detected divergence.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; GRC/Compliance; ML Platform Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0405-015 - Break-glass dual-authorization and tenant-scoped activation guardrails

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure break-glass access requires MFA or equivalent dual-authorization at activation and is tenant-scoped where applicable so emergency access is constrained to the minimum necessary boundary.

## Objective

Ensure emergency access does not become a broad privileged bypass by enforcing strong activation controls and limiting break-glass scope to the affected tenant or operational boundary.

## Rationale

Emergency access is high risk because it intentionally bypasses normal identity paths. Dual-authorization and tenant-scoped activation reduce the likelihood that a single actor can activate broad emergency privileges or cross tenant boundaries during an incident.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers activation controls, MFA or equivalent dual-authorization, and tenant-scoped emergency credentials where applicable.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Emergency access activation guardrails.
- Implementation Surface: MFA or equivalent dual-authorization at emergency access activation, tenant-scoped break-glass credentials or kubeconfigs, scoped issuance workflows, and activation approval records.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management

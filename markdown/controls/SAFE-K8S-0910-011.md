# SAFE-K8S-0910-011 - Cross-cluster model provenance preservation and federation endpoint governance

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Model registry federation across clusters must preserve provenance chain-of-custody metadata for replicated artifacts and must govern federation membership so that only authorized registry endpoints can join, remain in, or receive artifacts from the federation.

## Objective

Preserve artifact traceability across cluster boundaries and prevent unauthorized registry endpoints from participating in model distribution federations.

## Rationale

Replication can undermine supply-chain assurance if provenance metadata is stripped or if unauthorized endpoints can join the federation and receive or publish artifacts. Preserving chain-of-custody metadata and explicitly governing endpoint membership keeps replicated models traceable to their originating pipeline and limits federation participation to authorized clusters.

## Scope

Applies to federated model registries and cross-cluster artifact replication topologies that preserve model metadata, signing identity, promotion gate results, and authorized endpoint registries across clusters or regions.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Federation membership management and provenance preservation configuration for replicated model artifacts.
- Implementation Surface: Authorized federation endpoint registries, provenance metadata preservation settings, change-managed endpoint onboarding and revocation, replication drift reconciliation scans, and controls ensuring downstream clusters can trace replicated models to their originating training and promotion workflow.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering

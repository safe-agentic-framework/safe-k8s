# SAFE-K8S-0910-009 - Cross-cluster communication authorization and anomaly monitoring

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads must enforce policies that restrict communication to authorized workload pairs and must monitor cross-cluster traffic for anomalous sources, destinations, or transfer patterns that indicate misuse, lateral movement, or data exfiltration.

## Objective

Prevent unauthorized cross-cluster AI communication and detect suspicious traffic patterns that single-cluster network telemetry cannot expose.

## Rationale

Encrypted transport alone does not prevent any federated workload from talking to every other cluster endpoint. Cross-cluster authorization policies limit reachable peers, while traffic monitoring and anomaly detection expose misuse, lateral movement, and abnormal data transfer that would otherwise traverse inter-cluster links without visibility.

## Scope

Applies to all cross-cluster network communication for AI workloads, including distributed training gradient exchange, multi-cluster inference routing, and cross-cluster pipeline data transfer.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cross-cluster authorization policy enforcement, traffic telemetry collection, anomaly detection rules, and investigation workflows for inter-cluster communication.
- Implementation Surface: Cross-cluster AuthorizationPolicy or Cilium ClusterMesh network policies with default-deny and explicit allow rules, service identity based allowlists for authorized workload pairs, cross-cluster traffic dashboards and anomaly detection for unexpected sources and data volumes, and alerting and triage workflows for suspicious inter-cluster communication.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; ML Platform Engineering

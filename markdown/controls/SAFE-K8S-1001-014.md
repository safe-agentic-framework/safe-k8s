# SAFE-K8S-1001-014 - Centralized collection and segregation of AI workload logs

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.1 - Logging and Audit
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must centralize AI workload logs, including training job logs, inference service operational logs, and pipeline execution logs, into the enterprise logging platform and must preserve source, tenant, and workload context so those log streams remain searchable, segregated, and usable for investigation.

## Objective

Provide unified visibility into AI workload activity by collecting AI-specific operational log streams into the enterprise logging platform with sufficient tenant and workload context for investigation and governance.

## Rationale

AI workloads emit operational logs that are not fully captured by standard cluster logging. Without centralized collection and workload-aware labeling, investigators cannot reconstruct training failures, inference anomalies, or pipeline manipulation events, and multi-tenant platforms lose the ability to separate one tenant's log activity from another's.

## Scope

Applies to all AI/ML workloads running on Kubernetes, including training jobs, inference services, and ML pipeline controllers. Covers centralized collection, workload-aware labeling, and tenant-aware segregation of AI workload logs. Excludes the content-redaction logic applied to sensitive inference payloads before storage.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Log pipeline routing rules, workload metadata enrichment, and centralized logging platform ingestion configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Log pipeline collection rules for training, inference, and pipeline controllers; workload and tenant metadata enrichment; cloud logging service routing for AI workload logs; and centralized logging platform indexes or views that preserve log class, tenant, and workload context.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; Data Privacy

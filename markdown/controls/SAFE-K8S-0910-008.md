# SAFE-K8S-0910-008 - Cross-cluster transport encryption and workload authentication

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads must use encrypted channels and authenticated workload-to-workload or cluster-to-cluster endpoints so that gradient exchange, inference routing, and pipeline data transfer cannot be intercepted or spoofed across cluster boundaries.

## Objective

Protect cross-cluster AI communication from interception and endpoint impersonation by enforcing encrypted and authenticated transport across federated clusters.

## Rationale

Distributed AI traffic often crosses network paths and trust boundaries that single-cluster controls cannot protect. Requiring encrypted channels with authenticated endpoints prevents eavesdropping, tampering, and impersonation on cross-cluster links before higher-level authorization decisions are evaluated.

## Scope

Applies to all cross-cluster network communication for AI workloads, including distributed training gradient exchange, multi-cluster inference routing, and cross-cluster pipeline data transfer.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Multi-cluster service mesh mTLS configuration, VPN or tunnel endpoint authentication, certificate lifecycle controls, and transport security verification.
- Implementation Surface: Multi-cluster service mesh platforms such as Istio multi-cluster, Cilium ClusterMesh, or Linkerd multi-cluster with mTLS; VPN or WireGuard tunnels with SPIFFE-based endpoint authentication; certificate issuance, rotation, and validation for cross-cluster links; and transport verification for cross-cluster AI data paths.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; ML Platform Engineering

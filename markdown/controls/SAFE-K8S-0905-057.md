# SAFE-K8S-0905-057 - External model trust-signal assessment and approval review

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must review trust signals for externally sourced models before approving them for internal use and must document the resulting approval or rejection decision.

## Objective

Use explicit trust-signal assessment to determine whether an externally sourced model is acceptable for internal use after provenance data is gathered.

## Rationale

Provenance metadata alone does not resolve whether an externally sourced model is sufficiently trustworthy. Trust-signal review evaluates publisher reputation, maintenance signals, community confidence, and other contextual indicators needed to support an approval decision.

## Scope

Applies to all externally sourced models considered for internal use. Covers trust-signal review, approval rationale, and documented disposition decisions for externally sourced models. Excludes internal re-signing, ML-BOM external-origin annotation, and internal registry admission, which remain covered by SAFE-K8S-0905-031.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Trust review and disposition decision workflow before internal acceptance.
- Implementation Surface: Trust-signal review records for externally sourced models before approval, approval or rejection records documenting trust-based disposition decisions, and governance notes capturing the trust rationale for accepted external models.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

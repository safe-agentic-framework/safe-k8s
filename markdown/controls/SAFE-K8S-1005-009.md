# SAFE-K8S-1005-009 - Kubernetes containment procedures for nodes, namespaces, workloads, and credentials

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must define and document Kubernetes incident containment procedures for node draining, namespace isolation, workload suspension, and credential revocation, with pre-built runbooks and isolation templates that enable targeted containment while minimizing impact to unaffected workloads.

## Objective

Enable rapid, targeted containment of Kubernetes security incidents through documented and tested platform-level containment procedures that reduce blast radius without causing unnecessary disruption.

## Rationale

Kubernetes incidents often require containment actions that differ from traditional infrastructure response, such as node draining, namespace isolation, and credential revocation. Without documented procedures and pre-built templates, responders may either over-isolate and cause avoidable outages or under-isolate and fail to contain the incident.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters hosting AI workloads. Covers node-level, namespace-level, workload-level, and credential-level containment procedures, including multi-tenant environments where containment must preserve unaffected workloads wherever possible.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: IR playbook containment procedures, namespace isolation templates, and emergency authorization for Kubernetes containment actions.
- Implementation Surface: kubectl cordon and drain procedures, deny-all NetworkPolicy manifests with documented exceptions, workload suspension or scaling procedures, ServiceAccount and external credential revocation procedures, and tested runbooks for each containment layer.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering; SRE

# SAFE-K8S-0905-019 - ML-BOM generation and model lineage metadata capture

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0905-042

## Statement

Organizations must generate ML Bills of Materials for production-bound models that capture model provenance metadata including training data lineage, hyperparameters, framework versions, and training infrastructure identity, and must attach those ML-BOM records to model artifacts in a durable, reviewable form.

## Objective

Maintain auditable model lineage and supply-chain transparency so organizations can determine what data, software, and infrastructure produced a given model artifact.

## Rationale

Without ML-BOM generation, organizations cannot reliably trace which datasets, framework versions, and training conditions produced a model. That weakens impact analysis for vulnerability disclosures, provenance investigations, and data correction or deletion events.

## Scope

Applies to production-bound model artifacts generated or promoted through Kubernetes-connected AI delivery workflows. Covers CTA-2114-aligned ML-BOM generation, attachment to model artifacts, and preservation of model lineage metadata. Excludes generic software dependency scanning and deployment-time provenance verification.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: ML-BOM generation tooling integrated into training, registration, and artifact publication workflows.
- Implementation Surface: CTA-2114-aligned ML-BOM tooling, capture of model identity, training data lineage, hyperparameters, framework versions, training infrastructure metadata, and durable attachment of ML-BOM records to model artifacts or registries.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; DevOps Engineering

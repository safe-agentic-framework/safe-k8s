# SAFE-K8S-0903-005 - GPU inference autoscaling safety bounds and budget guardrails

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.3 - Inference Resilience, Adversarial Defense, and Resource Controls
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0903-007

## Statement

GPU-aware autoscaling for inference workloads must enforce maximum replica bounds, cooldown or stabilization settings, and budget-aware scale-up guardrails to prevent denial-of-wallet attacks.

## Objective

Prevent cost-based denial-of-service through uncontrolled GPU autoscaling by enforcing bounded, rate-limited, and budget-governed inference scale-out.

## Rationale

GPU-based inference pods are expensive to scale. Without hard replica ceilings, cooldown windows, and budget-linked suppression of additional scale-out, an attacker can drive runaway autoscaling and cause material cloud spend through denial-of-wallet attacks.

## Scope

Applies to all autoscaled inference deployments on Kubernetes using HPA, KEDA, or custom GPU-aware autoscalers. Covers replica bounds, stabilization or cooldown settings, scale-up guardrails, and budget-aware autoscaling controls. Excludes queue priority and timeout hardening, which are covered by SAFE-K8S-0903-006.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: HPA and KEDA resource definitions, autoscaler policy, custom metrics adapters, and FinOps budget integration.
- Implementation Surface: Kubernetes HorizontalPodAutoscaler and KEDA ScaledObject configurations with max replica bounds, stabilization or cooldown windows, controlled scale-up behavior, custom GPU utilization or queue metrics, tenant quota enforcement, and budget thresholds that disable further scale-up or trigger degraded service responses.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; FinOps

# SAFE-K8S-0910-023 - Cross-cluster traffic anomaly monitoring and investigation

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Cross-cluster network communication for distributed AI workloads must monitor traffic for anomalous sources, destinations, volumes, or transfer patterns and must investigate alerts that indicate misuse, lateral movement, or data exfiltration across cluster boundaries.

## Objective

Detect suspicious or abusive cross-cluster AI communication patterns that authorization policies alone may not prevent.

## Rationale

Even with explicit peer authorization, allowed communication paths can still be abused for misuse, lateral movement, or data exfiltration. Cross-cluster traffic monitoring and anomaly detection provide the visibility needed to identify abnormal sources, destinations, and transfer patterns that single-cluster telemetry may miss.

## Scope

Applies to all cross-cluster network communication for AI workloads, including distributed training gradient exchange, multi-cluster inference routing, and cross-cluster pipeline data transfer. Covers telemetry collection, anomaly detection, and alert investigation for inter-cluster communication. Excludes explicit authorization policy enforcement for allowed peers.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Cross-cluster traffic telemetry collection, anomaly detection, and alert investigation workflow.
- Implementation Surface: Cross-cluster traffic dashboards, anomaly detection for unexpected sources and data volumes, alerting and triage workflows for suspicious inter-cluster communication, and investigation records for suspected misuse or exfiltration over cross-cluster links.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; ML Platform Engineering

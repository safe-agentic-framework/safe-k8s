# SAFE-K8S-0905-027 - External model source enforcement through network policy and admission control

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must enforce the approved external model source allowlist through NetworkPolicy blocking direct pulls from public hubs to production namespaces and through admission controllers that reject pods pulling models from unapproved external sources.

## Objective

Prevent bypass of approved-source policy by enforcing source restrictions at runtime and admission.

## Rationale

Even with an allowlist, external source restrictions fail if production workloads can still pull directly from public hubs or if admission does not reject unapproved source references. Network and admission enforcement are required to make approved-source policy effective.

## Scope

Applies to production and protected Kubernetes namespaces that consume external models. Covers NetworkPolicy restrictions on public hub access and admission control validation of model source URLs or image references. Excludes quarantine and vetting workflow, which is covered by SAFE-K8S-0905-020 and SAFE-K8S-0905-021.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: NetworkPolicy enforcement point and admission controller policy.
- Implementation Surface: NetworkPolicy blocking direct public hub access from protected namespaces, admission controllers such as Kyverno or Gatekeeper rejecting unapproved external model sources, and enforcement telemetry showing denied pulls or rejected workloads.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Governance

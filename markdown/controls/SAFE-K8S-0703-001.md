# SAFE-K8S-0703-001 - Priority classes and preemption policies for AI workloads

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must define PriorityClass objects for system-critical, high-priority (production inference), default (training), and low-priority (development notebooks) workload tiers, and must configure preemption policies that protect production inference from being preempted by training jobs.

## Objective

Ensure that production inference workloads maintain availability during resource contention by establishing clear workload priority tiers with preemption protection.

## Rationale

Without defined priority classes, Kubernetes schedules all pods at the same priority, meaning a large training job can consume all available GPUs and leave inference services unable to scale. Without preemption policies, low-priority development notebooks may block high-priority production workloads. Without queue management (Kueue, Volcano), fair-share scheduling across tenants is not achievable, leading to GPU starvation for some teams and resource hoarding by others.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters with GPU resources or dedicated AI workloads. PriorityClass definitions must cover at minimum system-critical, inference-production, training-default, and development-low tiers. Queue management (Kueue or Volcano) is required for clusters shared across multiple teams submitting GPU workloads. Single- tenant dedicated clusters may use simplified priority schemes with documented justification.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: PriorityClass objects, preemption policy settings, and admission control policies ensuring pods specify a PriorityClassName.
- Implementation Surface: PriorityClass objects (scheduling.k8s.io/v1) with tiered value hierarchy (system-critical, inference-production, training-default, dev-notebooks), preemptionPolicy configuration (PreemptLowerPriority, Never), and admission control policies requiring PriorityClass specification on pods in AI namespaces and validating allowed tiers per namespace.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Engineering; FinOps

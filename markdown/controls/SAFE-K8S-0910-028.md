# SAFE-K8S-0910-028 - Unified multi-cluster compliance reporting

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters must aggregate compliance evidence and control status across the federation into unified reporting that presents a single multi-cluster view of security posture, exceptions, and audit coverage.

## Objective

Provide unified governance visibility across multi-cluster AI platforms by consolidating compliance evidence and exception status into a single reporting view.

## Rationale

Federated AI platforms become difficult to govern when compliance evidence remains fragmented by cluster. Unified multi-cluster reporting gives operators, auditors, and governance stakeholders a single view of which controls are enforced where, what exceptions exist, and how the aggregate posture changes across the federation.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters. Covers aggregation of compliance evidence, exception status, and audit coverage across cluster boundaries into a unified reporting view. Excludes centralized secret and certificate governance and cross-cluster incident coordination operations.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Unified reporting platform and cross-cluster compliance evidence aggregation process.
- Implementation Surface: Compliance evidence aggregation across clusters, normalized control-status dashboards, consolidated exception registers, and reporting workflows that present federation-wide audit posture rather than per-cluster views only.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; GRC/Compliance; ML Platform Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0910-010 - Cross-cluster registry replication channel authentication and signature re-verification

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Model registry replication across clusters must use authenticated and encrypted replication channels, and each destination cluster or registry endpoint must independently re-verify model signatures and replicated digests before accepting replicated artifacts.

## Objective

Prevent tampered model artifacts from traversing cross-cluster replication channels by securing the channel and requiring independent signature verification at every destination endpoint.

## Rationale

A secure source registry is not sufficient if downstream clusters trust replicated artifacts without their own verification. Authenticated replication channels reduce interception and spoofing risk, while signature and digest re-verification at each destination ensures a compromised endpoint cannot silently inject or alter model artifacts in transit or on arrival.

## Scope

Applies to model registry replication and federation channels spanning clusters or regions, including Harbor replication, OCI registry replication, and custom model distribution pipelines where destination clusters receive replicated artifacts from upstream registries.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Replication channel configuration and destination-side artifact verification policy at each replication endpoint.
- Implementation Surface: mTLS or equivalent authenticated replication channels, SPIFFE or other workload identity for replication endpoints, destination-side signature verification policy, content-addressable digest verification after replication, and operational controls that block acceptance of unverifiable replicated artifacts.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering

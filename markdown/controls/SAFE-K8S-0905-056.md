# SAFE-K8S-0905-056 - External model publisher identity and provenance metadata verification

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must verify available provenance metadata for externally sourced models, including publisher identity and available commit, release, or signature provenance, before the models are approved for internal use.

## Objective

Preserve chain of custody for external model intake by validating publisher identity and available provenance metadata before internal acceptance.

## Rationale

Even when a model passes malware or behavioral screening, deployment remains unsafe if the organization cannot establish who published it and what provenance metadata accompanies the artifact. Provenance verification provides the chain-of-custody context needed before internal use.

## Scope

Applies to all externally sourced models admitted for internal use. Covers publisher identity verification, model card or source metadata review, and commit-signature or release-signature provenance checks. Excludes broader trust-signal risk assessment and disposition review.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Provenance verification workflow before internal acceptance.
- Implementation Surface: Publisher identity checks, model card or source metadata review, commit-signature or release-signature validation, and documented provenance verification records for approved external models.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

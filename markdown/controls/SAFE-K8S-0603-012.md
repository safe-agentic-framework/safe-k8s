# SAFE-K8S-0603-012 - Lifecycle attestation verification and admission enforcement

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.3 - Attestation, Provenance, and Cryptographic Assurance
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must verify attestation chains at every lifecycle transition, including build, promote, and deploy, and must enforce policy-based admission decisions that reject artifacts lacking complete verified attestation chains.

## Objective

Ensure attestation checks are executed consistently across the artifact lifecycle and that deployment gates fail closed when required attestations are absent, invalid, or incomplete.

## Rationale

Well-written attestation policies do not reduce supply chain risk if no gate evaluates them at promotion or deployment time. Lifecycle verification and fail-closed admission enforcement prevent incomplete or stale artifacts from moving downstream and provide evidence that attestation requirements were actually enforced.

## Scope

Applies to all CI/CD pipelines, promotion workflows, and Kubernetes admission controllers that process signed artifact attestations from build through deployment.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: CI/CD gate evaluation and Kubernetes admission enforcement points.
- Implementation Surface: Policy engine integration into build, promotion, and deploy gates; attestation chain verification workflows; Kyverno or Sigstore policy-controller admission checks; and pass/fail enforcement logs across lifecycle stages.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: DevSecOps / Release Engineering; Platform Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0905-058 - Internal re-signing of approved external models before deployment eligibility

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must internally re-sign approved external models with organizational signing infrastructure before the models become deployable within organizational environments.

## Objective

Convert approved external artifacts into internally governed deployable assets through organizational cryptographic signing before deployment eligibility.

## Rationale

Using externally sourced models directly from outside repositories breaks the organization's trust boundary. Internal re-signing establishes organizational cryptographic custody and ensures that only internally governed artifacts become deployable.

## Scope

Applies to externally sourced models approved for internal use. Covers internal artifact signing and the requirement that only internally re-signed versions become deployable. Excludes external-origin annotation and registry enrollment recordkeeping.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Internal signing process before deployment eligibility.
- Implementation Surface: Internal artifact signing, signed external-model derivatives, and promotion logic that marks only internally re-signed external models as eligible for deployment.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

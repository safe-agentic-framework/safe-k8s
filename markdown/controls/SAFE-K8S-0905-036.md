# SAFE-K8S-0905-036 - Automated AI workload circuit-breaker threshold enforcement

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must define and enforce automated circuit-breaker thresholds that halt or isolate AI workloads when predefined safety, security, integrity, or resource-consumption conditions are breached. Automated circuit-breakers must execute through workload controllers, serving controls, or orchestration logic without requiring manual operator intervention.

## Objective

Contain unsafe or compromised AI workload behavior rapidly by ensuring automated threshold-based circuit-breakers trigger predictable enforcement actions.

## Rationale

AI workloads can cross safety, integrity, or resource-risk thresholds faster than operators can respond manually. Automated circuit-breaker enforcement limits blast radius when training jobs, inference services, or pipeline stages begin exhibiting anomalous or unsafe behavior.

## Scope

Applies to AI training jobs, inference services, and pipeline stages running on Kubernetes where automated threshold-triggered containment is implemented.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Automated workload controller and serving-layer enforcement that halts, scales down, or isolates workloads when configured thresholds are exceeded.
- Implementation Surface: Kubernetes Job limits, Deployment scale-to-zero or quarantine actions, HPA or KEDA triggers, service mesh circuit-breakers, custom controller health checks, and automated remediation policies.
- Owner Primary: AI/ML Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Operations; SRE

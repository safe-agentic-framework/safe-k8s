# SAFE-K8S-0905-020 - Public model quarantine scanning and sandbox vetting

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0905-044

## Statement

Organizations must route externally sourced models through an isolated quarantine workflow that scans for malicious payloads, validates model formats and structure, and performs sandboxed or behavioral vetting before those models are approved for internal use.

## Objective

Prevent malicious or unsafe third-party model artifacts from entering organizational infrastructure by requiring isolated scanning and vetting before ingestion.

## Rationale

Public model hubs routinely host unvetted artifacts. Without quarantine scanning and sandbox vetting, malicious pickle payloads, embedded files, unsafe formats, and backdoored model weights can move directly into training or serving environments.

## Scope

Applies to all models sourced from public hubs and community repositories. Covers quarantine namespaces, malware and format scanning, sandbox execution, behavioral checks, and documented disposition decisions. Excludes source allowlist governance, which is covered by SAFE-K8S-0905-013, and internal artifact signing, which is covered separately.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Quarantine namespace with isolated scanning and behavioral vetting pipeline.
- Implementation Surface: Quarantine Kubernetes namespace with restricted egress, automated format and payload scanning, sandboxed execution with monitoring, behavioral validation against standard datasets, and workflow records showing pass, fail, or reject decisions.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

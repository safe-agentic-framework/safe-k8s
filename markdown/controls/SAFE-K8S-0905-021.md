# SAFE-K8S-0905-021 - External model provenance verification and internal re-registration

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0905-030

## Statement

Organizations must verify available provenance metadata for externally sourced models and must internally re-sign, annotate, and re-register approved external models before they are deployable within organizational environments.

## Objective

Preserve chain of custody and organizational trust boundaries for external model intake by validating provenance signals and converting approved artifacts into internally governed assets.

## Rationale

Even when a model passes malware or behavioral screening, deployment remains unsafe if the organization cannot establish provenance context or if the artifact is used directly from an external source without internal signing and registry enrollment. Internal re-registration creates a controlled chain of custody.

## Scope

Applies to all externally sourced models admitted for internal use. Covers provenance review of model cards, publisher identity, commit or release signatures, internal re-signing, ML-BOM annotation of external origin, and internal registry enrollment. Excludes external source allowlist enforcement and pre-admission quarantine scanning.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Provenance review workflow and internal signing / registry admission process.
- Implementation Surface: Publisher identity checks, commit-signature review, trust-signal capture, internal artifact signing, ML-BOM annotation of external origin and scan results, and internal registry enrollment records for approved models.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0603-003 - Attestation policy evaluation and lifecycle compliance

- Domain: D06 - Supply Chain, Images, and Admission Control
- Knowledge Area: 6.3 - Attestation, Provenance, and Cryptographic Assurance
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must define signed attestation policies that encode artifact requirements for each pipeline stage, verify attestation chains at every lifecycle transition (build, promote, deploy), and enforce policy-based admission decisions that reject artifacts lacking complete verified attestation chains. Attestation policies must themselves be versioned, signed, and subject to change control.

## Objective

Ensure that attestation verification is not ad hoc but governed by formal, signed policies that are evaluated consistently at every pipeline stage and at admission time.

## Rationale

Generating attestations without systematically evaluating them against policies leaves verification gaps. An artifact could carry incomplete or stale attestations and still pass deployment if no policy engine enforces requirements. Without signed policies, an attacker who compromises the policy store can weaken requirements. Lifecycle compliance tracing ensures no pipeline stage is skipped or its attestation forged.

## Scope

Applies to all CI/CD pipelines and admission controllers that process attestations. Covers the full artifact lifecycle from build through registry promotion to cluster deployment. Policy definitions must be stored in version-controlled repositories with branch protection.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Policy engine integrated into CI/CD pipeline gates and Kubernetes admission controllers.
- Implementation Surface: Signed attestation policy documents stored as OCI artifacts in container registries, policy engine integration into CI/CD pipeline gates (build, promote, deploy stages), Kyverno and Sigstore policy-controller attestation predicate evaluation at Kubernetes admission, version-controlled policy repositories with branch protection and change control, attestation chain verification across lifecycle transitions, and policy signing and versioning infrastructure.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: DevSecOps / Release Engineering; Platform Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0905-030 - External model provenance verification and trust-signal review

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must verify available provenance metadata for externally sourced models before they are approved for internal use.

## Objective

Preserve chain of custody for external model intake by validating provenance signals and publisher trust context before internal acceptance.

## Rationale

Even when a model passes malware or behavioral screening, deployment remains unsafe if the organization cannot establish provenance context for the artifact. Provenance review provides the trust signals needed to decide whether an external model is eligible for internal use.

## Scope

Applies to all externally sourced models admitted for internal use. Covers provenance review of model cards, publisher identity, release or commit signatures, and trust-signal capture. Excludes internal re-signing, ML-BOM external-origin annotation, and internal registry admission, which are covered by SAFE-K8S-0905-031.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Provenance review workflow before internal acceptance.
- Implementation Surface: Publisher identity checks, commit-signature or release-signature review, trust-signal capture, and documented provenance verification records for approved external models.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

# SAFE-K8S-0503-008 - Ingress WAF integration and rate limiting

- Domain: D05 - Network Security and Communication
- Knowledge Area: 5.3 - Ingress, Egress, and DNS Hardening
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Ingress controllers protecting internet-facing services must integrate with a web application firewall for request filtering and must apply rate limiting controls to protect backend services from abuse, credential stuffing, and application-layer denial-of-service attacks.

## Objective

Reduce exploit delivery and volumetric abuse at the ingress layer by filtering malicious requests and throttling abusive traffic before it reaches backend services.

## Rationale

TLS alone does not stop injection payloads, malformed requests, or application-layer denial-of-service attacks. WAF policies and ingress rate limiting are separate safeguards that must be configured, tuned, and monitored to protect exposed services from abuse.

## Scope

Applies to internet-facing ingress controllers and to internal ingress paths where risk assessment requires request filtering or abuse throttling. Covers WAF integrations, request filtering rules, and per-route or per-client rate limiting.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Ingress Filtering / Abuse Protection
- Implementation Surface: WAF policy configuration, ingress annotations or CRDs for rate limiting, and detection of abusive traffic patterns.
- Owner Primary: Network Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Application Security; Platform Engineering

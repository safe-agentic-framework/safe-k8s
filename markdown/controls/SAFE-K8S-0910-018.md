# SAFE-K8S-0910-018 - Security-aware target-cluster posture verification before multi-cluster placement

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.10 - Multi-Cluster and Federated AI Security
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Multi-cluster AI workload orchestration control planes must verify target-cluster health and security posture before workload placement and must block placement to clusters that do not meet defined security eligibility criteria.

## Objective

Ensure that AI workloads are placed only on clusters that currently satisfy required security and health baselines.

## Rationale

Multi-cluster AI platforms inherit the weakest-cluster problem. Without placement-time posture verification, workloads can be routed onto clusters with unsupported Kubernetes versions, disabled admission controls, unsafe GPU driver baselines, or unresolved critical security findings.

## Scope

Applies to organizations operating AI workloads across multiple Kubernetes clusters, whether on-premises, multi-cloud, or hybrid. Covers target-cluster posture verification before workload placement, including health and security eligibility checks. Excludes compromised-cluster isolation, workload draining, and re-admission after isolation.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Security-aware placement policy definitions and target-cluster posture assessment feeds used before workload placement.
- Implementation Surface: Security-aware placement policies, target-cluster posture assessment feeds such as SAFE-K8S-1007-004 outputs, cluster eligibility decision logic, and placement decision logs recording security justification for cluster selection or rejection.
- Owner Primary: Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: ML Platform Engineering; Security Engineering; SRE

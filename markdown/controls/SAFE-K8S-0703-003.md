# SAFE-K8S-0703-003 - GPU cost attribution and chargeback mechanisms

- Domain: D07 - Storage, Multi-tenancy, and Resource Governance
- Knowledge Area: 7.3 - Resource Governance and Priority
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement per-namespace, per-team, and per-job GPU cost attribution and chargeback mechanisms to provide visibility into GPU consumption and enable accountability for resource usage.

## Objective

Provide granular visibility into GPU consumption at the namespace, team, and job level to enable cost accountability and chargeback reporting.

## Rationale

GPU resources are among the most expensive components in AI infrastructure. Without cost attribution, teams have no accountability for consumption. Abandoned training jobs, over-provisioned inference endpoints, and reserved-but-unused GPUs can accumulate thousands of dollars in daily waste. Without spending limits and automatic enforcement, a single misconfigured job or runaway training loop can exhaust an organization's entire GPU budget before manual intervention occurs.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters with GPU resources. Cost attribution must cover per-namespace, per-team, and per-job granularity. Idle detection must monitor GPU utilization and flag resources below configurable thresholds for configurable time periods. Spending limits and automatic enforcement must be configured at the namespace or team level. FinOps integration is required for chargeback reporting.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: GPU monitoring and cost attribution tooling (e.g., Kubecost, OpenCost) integrated with cloud billing and Kubernetes namespace and label dimensions.
- Implementation Surface: Cloud-native cost allocation tagging (AWS Cost Explorer tags, GCP labels, Azure tags) mapped to Kubernetes namespace and label dimensions, Kubernetes cost monitoring tools (OpenCost, Kubecost) for per-namespace GPU cost tracking, and chargeback reporting pipelines.
- Owner Primary: FinOps
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; ML Engineering

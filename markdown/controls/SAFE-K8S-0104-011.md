# SAFE-K8S-0104-011 - CIS Benchmark remediation tracking and exception governance

- Domain: D01 - Control Plane and Cluster Hardening
- Knowledge Area: 1.4 - CIS Benchmarks and Patch Management
- Maturity: Foundational
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Failing CIS Kubernetes Benchmark checks must be tracked through a documented remediation workflow with ownership and resolution timelines, and any accepted exceptions must be formally approved by a risk owner, documented with justification and compensating controls, and re-reviewed on a defined schedule.

## Objective

Ensure benchmark findings produce accountable remediation or explicitly governed risk acceptance rather than unmanaged backlog.

## Rationale

Benchmark scans only improve security when failing checks drive timely corrective action or formally approved exceptions. Untracked findings and undocumented exceptions create hidden risk and undermine the value of the baseline assessment program.

## Scope

Applies to all production clusters and to all failing or waived CIS Benchmark checks identified through recurring assessment.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Workflow / Governance
- Implementation Surface: Issue tracking, remediation management, and exception approval workflow tied to CIS Benchmark findings.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Governance Risk and Compliance

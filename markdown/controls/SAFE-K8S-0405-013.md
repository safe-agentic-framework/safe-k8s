# SAFE-K8S-0405-013 - Break-glass recovery procedure definition and testing

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must define and periodically test break-glass emergency access procedures that allow authorized personnel to regain cluster access when normal authentication pathways are unavailable.

## Objective

Ensure administrators can recover cluster access during identity-provider outages or incidents without resorting to ad hoc workarounds.

## Rationale

When identity systems fail, operators may improvise with unsafe shared credentials or disabled controls. A defined and tested emergency recovery path preserves administrative continuity without relying on unverified improvisation.

## Scope

Applies to production Kubernetes clusters that depend on OIDC providers or external identity systems for normal administrative access. Covers documented runbooks, authorized activation steps, storage and retrieval workflow, and periodic validation exercises.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Documented and tested emergency access workflow for authentication outages.
- Implementation Surface: Break-glass runbooks, controlled credential retrieval process, short-lived emergency kubeconfigs or client certificates, and scheduled exercises demonstrating access restoration during outage scenarios.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management

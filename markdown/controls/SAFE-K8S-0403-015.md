# SAFE-K8S-0403-015 - Secret leak incident response and credential revocation

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.3 - Secrets Management
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must implement incident response procedures for secret leak events that drive rapid credential revocation, replacement, containment, and documented response actions when leaked secrets are detected.

## Objective

Ensure leaked credentials are rapidly revoked and replaced through a defined response workflow so exposed secrets do not remain usable after detection.

## Rationale

Detection alone does not reduce risk if exposed credentials remain valid. A defined response workflow with revocation timing, replacement steps, and documented containment limits the exploitation window for leaked credentials.

## Scope

Applies to any detected exposure of credentials, tokens, keys, or secrets in repositories, images, CI/CD artifacts, logs, and runtime outputs. Covers response initiation, containment, revocation, replacement, and incident documentation.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Incident response and credential lifecycle processes triggered by secret leak findings.
- Implementation Surface: Secret leak triage workflows, credential revocation and reissuance procedures, containment actions, notification and escalation paths, and incident records showing response timing.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Application Development

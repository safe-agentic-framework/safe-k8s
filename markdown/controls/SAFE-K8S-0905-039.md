# SAFE-K8S-0905-039 - Model registry access review and stale-permission remediation

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.5 - AI Supply Chain and Model Lifecycle
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must perform periodic reviews of model registry access for human, service, and workload identities to identify excessive, stale, or mis-scoped permissions and must remediate identified entitlement issues within a defined governance process.

## Objective

Reduce lingering or excessive registry privileges by requiring periodic entitlement review and remediation for model registry access.

## Rationale

Even when registry RBAC is designed correctly at initial provisioning, privileges drift over time as projects change, workloads are retired, and emergency access accumulates. Periodic access review and timely remediation are required to keep registry entitlements aligned with current operational need.

## Scope

Applies to all model registry deployments on Kubernetes, including Harbor, MLflow Model Registry, and on-premises Hugging Face Hub instances, wherever human or machine identities retain standing access to model repositories.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Periodic registry entitlement review and remediation workflow.
- Implementation Surface: Registry access review records, identity and repository entitlement inventories, stale-permission removal workflows, and governance procedures for remediation of excessive access.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; Platform Engineering

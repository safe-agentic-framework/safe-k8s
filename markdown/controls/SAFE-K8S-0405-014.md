# SAFE-K8S-0405-014 - Break-glass access auditing, dual-authorization, and automatic expiration

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure all break-glass access is fully audited, requires MFA or equivalent dual-authorization at activation, is tenant-scoped where applicable, and is automatically time-limited or revoked after the emergency window.

## Objective

Ensure emergency access does not become an unbounded privileged backdoor by enforcing accountability, constrained scope, and automatic expiry.

## Rationale

Emergency access is high risk because it intentionally bypasses normal identity paths. Auditability, dual-authorization, tenant scoping, and automatic expiration preserve accountability and limit the blast radius of exceptional access.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers activation controls, audit trails, tenant scoping, and expiration or revocation after use.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Emergency access guardrails and accountability controls applied at activation and throughout the emergency window.
- Implementation Surface: Audit logging for emergency access usage, MFA or dual-authorization at activation, tenant-scoped emergency credentials, short TTLs, automatic revocation or expiration policies, and post-use access review.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management

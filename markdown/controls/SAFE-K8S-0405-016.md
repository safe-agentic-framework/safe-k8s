# SAFE-K8S-0405-016 - Break-glass access audit logging and automatic expiration

- Domain: D04 - Identity, Access, and Secrets Management
- Knowledge Area: 4.5 - Identity Abuse Detection and Mitigation
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: baseline

## Statement

Organizations must ensure all break-glass access is fully audited and is automatically time-limited, revoked, or expired after the emergency window.

## Objective

Ensure emergency access remains accountable and cannot persist as an unbounded privileged backdoor after the incident response window closes.

## Rationale

Break-glass access creates an intentional exception to normal controls. Audit trails and automatic expiration are required to preserve accountability, support post-use review, and ensure exceptional privileges do not silently become standing access.

## Scope

Applies to all break-glass credentials, kubeconfigs, or certificates issued for production Kubernetes clusters, including managed and multi-tenant environments. Covers audit trails, post-use review evidence, automatic revocation, and expiration after emergency access use.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Emergency access accountability and expiry controls.
- Implementation Surface: Audit logging for emergency access usage, short TTLs, automatic revocation or expiration policies, and post-use access review or reconciliation evidence.
- Owner Primary: Security Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; Identity and Access Management

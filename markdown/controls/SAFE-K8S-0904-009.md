# SAFE-K8S-0904-009 - Experiment tracking metadata access control and audit logging

- Domain: D09 - AI Workload Security: Training, Serving, and Pipelines
- Knowledge Area: 9.4 - AI Pipeline Orchestration and Experimentation Security
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific
- Superseded By: SAFE-K8S-0904-015

## Statement

Experiment tracking systems must enforce access controls on experiment metadata, hyperparameters, metrics, and run history, and must log access and modification activity to prevent unauthorized disclosure or manipulation.

## Objective

Protect the confidentiality and integrity of experiment tracking data by restricting access to experiment metadata and preserving auditable records of access and change activity.

## Rationale

Experiment metadata reveals proprietary model configurations, hyperparameters, performance metrics, and fine-tuning decisions. Without strong access control and auditability, insiders or external attackers can harvest sensitive ML intellectual property or tamper with experimental results without clear attribution.

## Scope

Applies to experiment tracking platforms such as MLflow and Weights & Biases deployed on Kubernetes or used by Kubernetes-based ML platforms. Covers RBAC, project or run-level access control, tenant isolation for experiment metadata, and audit logging for experiment access and modification events. Excludes pipeline definition signing and rollback integrity, which are covered by SAFE-K8S-0904-010.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Experiment tracking server access policy and audit log configuration.
- Implementation Surface: Experiment tracking platform authentication and authorization settings, project and run-level RBAC, tenant-scoped metadata visibility controls, and audit logging for read, write, and administrative operations on experiment records.
- Owner Primary: ML Platform Engineering
- Owner Supporting: Security Engineering; ML Engineering

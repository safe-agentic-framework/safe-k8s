# SAFE-K8S-1006-002 - SSDF v1.1 and SP 800-218A alignment for Kubernetes AI development

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.6 - Compliance and Governance
- Maturity: Advanced
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must map their Kubernetes and AI/ML development security practices to NIST SSDF v1.1 (SP 800-218) requirements and the NIST SP 800-218A AI/ML-specific community profile, identifying alignment and gaps across the Prepare, Protect the Software, Produce Well-Secured Software, and Respond to Vulnerabilities practice groups, with SP 800-218A providing the authoritative AI-focused adaptations for training data integrity, model provenance, and ML pipeline security.

## Objective

Ensure that secure development practices for Kubernetes AI workloads meet the NIST SSDF standard, providing a defensible baseline for software supply chain security and regulatory compliance.

## Rationale

AI and ML development introduces unique secure development challenges, such as training data validation, model provenance, and experiment reproducibility, that are not addressed by default SSDF interpretations. Without explicit SSDF alignment adapted for AI and ML patterns, organizations cannot demonstrate to regulators or customers that their AI development practices meet federal secure software development expectations, which are increasingly required in government procurement and regulated industries.

## Scope

Applies to all software development and ML engineering teams building or deploying AI workloads on Kubernetes. Covers the full SSDF v1.1 practice group set with AI/ML-specific adaptations. Must be reviewed when SSDF is updated or when AI development toolchain changes significantly.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Secure development policy documentation, CI/CD pipeline compliance checks, and SSDF self-attestation process.
- Implementation Surface: NIST SSDF v1.1 (SP 800-218) practice group alignment documentation (Prepare, Protect the Software, Produce Well-Secured Software, Respond to Vulnerabilities), NIST SP 800-218A AI/ML community profile task identifiers and AI-specific adaptations (training data as source code, training runs as builds, model weight integrity as software integrity), alignment mapping to SAFE-K8S controls across practice groups (PO.1 to CI/CD pipeline security, PS.1 to branch protection and signed commits, PW.6 to reproducible/hermetic pipeline execution, RV.1 to vulnerability scanning and CVE feeds), and gap identification documentation for AI-specific development practices.
- Owner Primary: GRC/Compliance
- Owner Supporting: AI/ML Engineering; DevOps/Platform Engineering; Security Architecture

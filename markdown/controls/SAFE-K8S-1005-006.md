# SAFE-K8S-1005-006 - Lateral movement prevention and component integrity verification during recovery

- Domain: D10 - Observability, Incident Response, and Governance
- Knowledge Area: 10.5 - Incident Response for Kubernetes
- Maturity: Practitioner
- Classification: ai-specific

## Statement

Organizations must implement controls to prevent lateral movement from compromised AI workloads to the cluster control plane or other tenants, and must verify component integrity before restoration to prevent reinfection during recovery operations.

## Objective

Ensure that recovery operations do not reintroduce compromised components or enable attacker persistence through lateral movement from compromised AI workloads.

## Rationale

Compromised AI workloads with elevated privileges (GPU access, storage mounts) can serve as pivot points for lateral movement to the control plane or other tenants if not properly isolated during and after recovery. Restoring components without integrity verification risks reinfecting the environment.

## Scope

Applies to all Kubernetes clusters recovering from security incidents affecting AI workloads. Covers lateral movement prevention controls, component integrity checks during recovery, and protection of cluster control plane and co-tenant workloads from compromised AI workload pivot.

## Enforcement

- Primary Enforcement Point: Post-recovery validation checklists, RBAC and NetworkPolicy enforcement for AI workload isolation, and component integrity verification tooling.
- Implementation Surface: RBAC least-privilege verification for AI workload ServiceAccounts, NetworkPolicy segmentation between AI workloads and control plane, container image re-pull from trusted registry (bypassing local node cache), node OS integrity verification, and credential rotation for all secrets accessible from compromised components.
- Owner Primary: Security Operations
- Owner Supporting: Platform Engineering; AI/ML Engineering
